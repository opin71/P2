Digital projects covers a wide scope of areas and each has it's own group of development and testing tools alongside their project management.

I have completed the Linkedin Agile Foundations, How to become an Agile Project Manager as well transitioning between Waterfall and Agile courses subsequently the below is what I learnt from the above courses, even though I have worked on a number of Waterfall projects in business development in the private sector and academic research projects in the public sector.

Firstly you need to ascertain what type of project methodology you will use, whether it will be Waterfall and/or Agile, there can be a mix of both. If it is a project that requires short turnarounds and revision of a deliverable every two weeks e.g gaming app then Agile would be the best project methodology to use and if you were doing 3 year academic project then Waterfall methodology would be the best to use because you would be working with milestones that would be 6 month to 1 year and a deliverable would be expected after 3 years.

There are quite a few Agile tools out there e.g Microsoft Excel, Atlassian Trello and JIRA, VersionOne, Microsoft Team Foundation Server (TFS), CA Agile Central (formerly Rally) and Agility Health.

In terms of Waterfall project management tools Microsoft Project and Excel, FinancialForce (SalesForce) and others can be used.

In terms of what makes a project successful and to be brief, an efficient and effective time management approach along with scoping a project adequately from the beginning whilst being flexible, having contingency strategies and planning e.g. factoring in risk and having options to address those earlier on in the project.

In summary a successful project from beginning to deliverable needs to have the right tools, a clear outline of the timelines and deliverables, a cohesive team approach, a succinct team brief and clear cohesive team communication strategies and channels.
